Inspector Toggles — Three Approaches

How should Tasks, Review, and Agents surface in the instance detail view?

A

Appear on Demand

Hide the toggle buttons entirely by default. They only appear when there's actual content — Tasks shows when the AI creates todos, Review shows when a review completes, Agents shows when children spawn. When they do appear, they animate in with a subtle notification pulse so you notice.

Pros

  • Zero visual noise when not relevant (most of the time)
  • Appearing = something happened, creates natural attention
  • Simplest change — just add conditional rendering

Cons

  • No way to manually check "do I have tasks?" — they're invisible until populated
  • If you miss the appear animation, you might not notice
B

Floating Status Chips

Replace the toggle bar with small floating chips anchored to the bottom-right of the output stream. They only materialize when relevant, slide in from the right, and show a compact summary (e.g., "3/7 tasks" or "Review: 2 issues"). Clicking expands an overlay panel. When empty, nothing is there at all.

Pros

  • Zero noise when empty — chips don't exist
  • Glanceable status without clicking (counts visible on chip)
  • Doesn't disrupt the chat → composer flow
  • Feels modern and non-intrusive

Cons

  • More complex positioning (floating/overlay)
  • Could overlap with output content at edge of scroll
C

Inline in Output Stream

Remove the toggles completely. Instead, render task lists and review results as special message blocks directly in the chat output stream, right where the AI created them. Tasks appear as a live-updating card inline with the conversation. Review results appear as a findings card after the review message.

Pros

  • Tasks/reviews appear in context — you see them where they were created
  • No separate panel or toggle to manage
  • Follows the natural conversation flow
  • Eliminates the inspector concept entirely

Cons

  • Task card scrolls away as conversation continues — need a "pin to top" or scroll-back
  • Bigger architectural change (new output message types)
  • Review panel has interactive controls — harder to embed inline

My recommendation: A — it solves both problems (noise + empty state) with the smallest change. The toggles already work fine mechanically; they just shouldn't be visible when there's nothing to toggle.
